How we predict your risk level

From the quick survey we take factors such as age, height,weight etc. from the user. From an already defined dataset of patients who had heart diseases – which is our training set, we can make use of models which can estimate some patterns for predictions and we can predict whether one is at risk or not. This is done using a specific machine learning algorithm –

Random Forrest Classifier.

Random forest, consists of a large number ofindividual decision trees that operate as an ensemble. Each individual tree in the random forest spits out a class prediction and the class with the most votes becomes our model’s prediction.

Why use Machine learning

Machine Learning deals with big data and statistics helps us in terms of giving us smart alternatives to analyzing huge chunks of data. ML produces accurate results by developing fast and efficient algorithms.

A key machine learning benefit concerns this technology’s ability to review large volumes of data and identify patterns and trends that might not be apparent to a human. For instance, a machine learning program may successfully pinpoint a causal relationship between two events. This makes the technology highly effective at data mining, particularly on a continual, ongoing basis, as would be required for an algorithm. The ability to quickly and accurately identify trends or patterns is one of the key advantages of machine learning.


One of the chief ML applications in healthcare is the identification and diagnosis of diseases and ailments which are otherwise considered hard-to-diagnose. This can include anything from cancers which are tough to catch during the initial stages, to other genetic diseases
